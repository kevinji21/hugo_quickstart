## Summary
The Palo Alto City Council held its annual reorganization meeting on January 5, 2026, to elect its leadership for the upcoming year. The meeting saw the unanimous election of a new Mayor and a robust discussion regarding nominations for the Vice Mayor position, outlining key priorities for the city in the year ahead.

*   Councilmember Veenker was unanimously elected as Palo Alto's Mayor for 2026.
*   Mayor Veenker shared her key priorities, including accelerating housing initiatives, advancing climate action goals, and progressing the Cubberley Community Center project.
*   Councilmembers nominated Councilmember Stone and Councilmember Reckdahl for the position of Vice Mayor, leading to a discussion of their respective qualifications and experience.

## Agenda Highlights

**1. Election of the 2026 Mayor**
Outgoing Mayor Lauing opened the meeting by thanking residents and city staff for their support during his term. He then nominated Councilmember Veenker for Mayor, praising her leadership, people skills, and judgment. Other Councilmembers echoed these sentiments, highlighting her work on the Cubberley Community Center, addressing oversized vehicles, fostering community unity in the wake of the October 7 attacks, and her commitment to climate action and regional boards. Following public comment, Councilmember Veenker was unanimously elected as Mayor for 2026 and took the Oath of Office.

In her remarks, Mayor Veenker outlined her vision for the year. Her top priorities include making progress on housing, such as opening Project Homekey before summer, seeking proposals for affordable senior housing behind Avenidas, and securing a state "Prohousing" designation to access more affordable housing funds. She also committed to including carbon neutrality in the city's new two-year work plan and announced a conference with Stanford University on sustainable data centers. Finally, Mayor Veenker emphasized moving forward with a resident-driven proposal for the Cubberley Community Center to be placed on the November ballot.

**2. Election of the 2026 Vice Mayor**
Mayor Veenker then invited nominations for the 2026 Vice Mayor. Councilmember Lu nominated Councilmember Stone, noting his strong voter support, extensive work on housing, water, environmental issues, mental health initiatives, and his representation on various regional bodies. Councilmember Lu also mentioned the Council's tradition of the Vice Mayor often becoming Mayor the following year.

Councilmember Lythcott-Haims nominated Councilmember Reckdahl, emphasizing his wisdom, collaborative style, and deep experience across numerous city commissions and committees, including his astute approach to the city's budget and his enthusiasm for improving retail districts. Other Councilmembers weighed in, with some supporting Councilmember Stone for his broad experience and established relationships, and others supporting Councilmember Reckdahl for his leadership style, problem-solving abilities, and focus on community service. The discussion highlighted the strong qualifications of both nominees.
