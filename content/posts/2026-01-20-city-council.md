## Summary
The Palo Alto City Council held a special meeting on January 20, 2026, where they honored a long-serving city employee, heard public comments on various local issues, received updates from Councilmembers, and reviewed the results of the 2025 Annual Community Survey, which highlighted high resident satisfaction with city services but ongoing concerns about housing affordability.

*   Recognized Aaron (Bunk) Miller for 34 years of dedicated service to the Palo Alto Regional Water Quality Control Plant.
*   Heard public comments advocating for full electrification, promoting health education on natural gas risks, celebrating community service, and raising concerns about development safety and tree protection.
*   Received updates on Palo Alto Community Child Care's 50th anniversary and revised Caltrain corridor crossing strategies.
*   Reviewed the 2025 Annual Community Survey results, showing high resident satisfaction with City services, but persistent concerns about housing affordability and limited awareness of sustainability programs.

## Agenda Highlights

*   **Proclamation honoring Aaron (Bunk) Miller for 34 Years of Service:** The Council presented a proclamation to Aaron (Bunk) Miller, recognizing his 34 years of exemplary service at the Palo Alto Regional Water Quality Control Plant. He was praised for his leadership, mentorship, and dedication to ensuring the plant's operations, especially during challenging times like the 2022 New Year's Eve storm.

*   **Public Comment:** Residents spoke on several topics. Comments included calls for the City to accelerate its transition from natural gas to electricity, support for public education on the health impacts of natural gas stoves, appreciation for the recent Martin Luther King, Jr. Day celebration, and concerns regarding a proposed development's safety implications and the need for stronger tree protection policies.

*   **Council Member Questions, Comments and Announcements:** Councilmembers shared updates. One acknowledged the 50th anniversary of Palo Alto Community Child Care (PACCC). Another reported on Caltrain's updated corridor crossing strategy, which now emphasizes a multifaceted approach including select grade separations, crossing closures, and enhanced at-grade safety measures due to revised projections and funding changes. The Mayor thanked participants in the MLK Jr. Day celebration.

*   **Presentation of the 2025 Annual Community Survey Results:** Assistant City Manager Kiely Nosé and Polco EVP Sonya Wytinck presented the findings of the 2025 Community Survey. The survey indicated a high quality of life and strong resident satisfaction with most City services, including parks, libraries, and utilities. However, housing affordability and the cost of living remained the lowest-rated issues. The survey also highlighted limited resident awareness of sustainability programs and that financial incentives are key motivators for electrification. Councilmembers discussed specific findings, including a discrepancy in crime reporting in one area. No action was taken as this was a study session.
