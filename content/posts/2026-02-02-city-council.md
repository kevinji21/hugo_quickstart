## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held a regular meeting on February 2, 2026, covering a range of topics from community recognition to future development plans and important city-wide discussions. The Council honored a long-serving employee, received an early look at a significant housing proposal, and discussed updates from a national mayors' conference, including local impacts of artificial intelligence and immigration enforcement.

Key actions and discussions included:

*   **Honored Christopher Fujimoto** for 25 years of dedicated service to the City's Watershed Protection Group.
*   **Reviewed a prescreening request** for a proposed development at 44 and 88 Encina Avenue, which could bring 158 new rental housing units.
*   **Discussed updates from the U.S. Conference of Mayors**, focusing on the local impacts of AI data centers and concerns regarding immigration enforcement.
*   **Heard about potential new partners** for the Cubberley Community Center.
*   **No reportable action** was taken following a Closed Session regarding existing litigation.
*   One agenda item was deferred to a future date due to staff unavailability.

## Agenda Highlights

**1. Proclamation Honoring Christopher Fujimoto for 25 Years of Service to the City of Palo Alto**
The City Council recognized Christopher Fujimoto for his quarter-century of service, particularly his foundational role in developing the City's award-winning stormwater pollution-prevention programs within the Watershed Protection Group. His work included inspecting sites to protect local creeks and the San Francisco Bay from pollutants, leading creek cleanups, and establishing trash booms. Mr. Fujimoto is retiring, and the Council expressed gratitude for his significant contributions to the community and environment. No formal action was required as this was a recognition.

**2. Conference with Legal Counsel—Existing Litigation**
The Council met in a Closed Session to discuss existing litigation concerning "Sami Gerges vs Yolanda Franco Clausen, City of Palo Alto." Upon returning to the public meeting, Mayor Veenker announced that no reportable action was taken during the Closed Session.

**Council Member Questions, Comments and Announcements**
Mayor Veenker reported on her attendance at the U.S. Conference of Mayors, highlighting two dominant issues: the local impacts of Artificial Intelligence (AI) data centers (e.g., water use, energy demand, land use) and immigration enforcement. Mayors nationwide expressed concern over community fear and disruption related to immigration enforcement. Mayor Veenker clarified that Palo Alto is not seeking conflict, has no current information indicating federal immigration enforcement activity in the city, and that California law prohibits local law enforcement from cooperating with federal immigration agents. The City is preparing guidance for employees on appropriate responses and may consider joining a legal filing challenging federal efforts to compel local cooperation.

Vice Mayor Stone echoed concerns about fear in immigrant communities and emphasized the City's commitment to protecting residents within its legal authority. Councilmember Lythcott-Haims supported considering joining the legal filing and commended staff for a successful community open house regarding support strategies for RV dwellers. She also announced that TheatreWorks, a regional theater company, is interested in partnering with the City to relocate to the Cubberley Community Center, potentially becoming a second partner for the site.

**3. 44 and 88 Encina Avenue [25PLN-00308]. Request for Council Prescreening to Rezone the Subject Properties**
The Council held a study session to review an early proposal for a development at 44 and 88 Encina Avenue. The developer is seeking to rezone the properties from Community Commercial to a special Planned Home Zoning category to allow for the construction of 158 rental housing units and 315 parking spaces in two seven-story buildings. The proposed project, located in the north parking lot of Town & Country Village, would exceed current zoning standards for height, lot coverage, and density. This prescreening was for early Council feedback only, with no formal action or opinions formed at this stage.
