## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held a Special Meeting on March 23, 2026, addressing a variety of city matters. Key discussions included recognizing Women's History Month, approving essential city contracts for disaster preparedness and technology services, and advancing a significant policy to combat discrimination against women. The Council also dedicated time to interview candidates for important city commissions.

Key actions and discussions included:

*   Recognizing March as Women's History Month with a formal proclamation.
*   Approving an agreement with the American Red Cross for disaster shelters, including provisions for pets.
*   Authorizing a three-year contract for the city's email security and archiving services.
*   Extending and upgrading the MyCPAU online customer portal for utility services for another three years.
*   Holding the first reading of an ordinance to adopt local policies based on the United Nations Convention on the Elimination of all forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW).
*   Interviewing candidates for vacancies on the Human Relations, Planning & Transportation, and Utilities Advisory Commissions.

## Agenda Highlights

*   **Proclamation Recognizing March as Women's History Month:** The Council formally acknowledged and celebrated March as Women's History Month.
*   **Approval of Minutes from March 9, 2026 Meeting:** The Council approved the official record of their previous meeting held on March 9, 2026.
*   **Approval of a Facility Agreement and Pet Addendum with the American Red Cross:** The Council approved an agreement allowing the American Red Cross to use designated city facilities as shelters during disasters, with an important addition to accommodate pets.
*   **Approval of Mimecast Subscription Services:** The Council approved a three-year purchase order for Mimecast subscription services, which provide email security, archiving, and technical support for the city. The total cost for these services will not exceed $281,093 over three years.
*   **Approval of Contract Amendment for MyCPAU Customer Portal:** The Council approved an amendment to extend the contract with Smart Energy Water Inc. for an additional three years. This ensures continued licensing and upgrades of the MyCPAU online customer portal, which residents use for utility services, bringing the revised total contract amount to $2,002,771.
*   **FIRST READING: Adoption of an Ordinance to Implement CEDAW Principles:** The Council held the first reading of a proposed ordinance to amend the Palo Alto Municipal Code. This ordinance aims to adopt local policies that align with the foundational principles of the United Nations Convention on the Elimination of all forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW), as recommended by the Policy and Services Committee.
*   **Interview Candidates for Commission Vacancies:** The Council conducted interviews with individuals seeking to fill open positions on the Human Relations Commission, Planning & Transportation Commission, and Utilities Advisory Commission.
