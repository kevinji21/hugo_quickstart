## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held a special meeting on January 15, 2026, primarily to conduct a Closed Session. During this private session, the Council discussed matters related to the appointment of a City Attorney and labor negotiations concerning that position. Upon returning to open session, Mayor Veenker announced that no reportable action was taken. The meeting was brief, lasting just over an hour.

Key actions and discussion items:

*   The City Council convened a special meeting.
*   The majority of the meeting was spent in a Closed Session.
*   Topics discussed in Closed Session included the potential appointment of a City Attorney and related labor negotiations.
*   No public decisions or actions were announced following the Closed Session.
*   The meeting adjourned shortly after the Closed Session concluded.

## Agenda Highlights

**Closed Session**
The City Council went into a Closed Session, which is a private meeting allowed by state law for specific sensitive topics like personnel matters or labor negotiations. This ensures privacy for individuals and strategic discussions.

**1. Public Employee Appointment (City Attorney)**
During the Closed Session, the Council discussed the appointment of a public employee, specifically for the position of City Attorney. This discussion was held privately as allowed by government code for personnel matters. No public decision was made or announced regarding this appointment.

**2. Conference with Labor Negotiators (City Attorney)**
Also in Closed Session, the Council met with labor negotiators to discuss the terms of employment for an unrepresented employee, identified as the City Attorney. This private discussion is permitted by law for labor negotiation strategy. No public outcome or agreement was announced after this discussion.
