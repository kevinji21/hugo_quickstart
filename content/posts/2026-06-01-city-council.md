## Summary
The Palo Alto City Council held its regular meeting on June 1, 2026, addressing a range of city business from community recognition to significant infrastructure and policy decisions. Key discussions included a study session on license plate reader technology, while the Council also made important decisions regarding housing policy and various city contracts.

Key actions and discussions included:
*   Proclaimed June 2026 as LGBTQIA+ Pride Month.
*   Held a study session on Automated License Plate Recognition (ALPR) technology.
*   Indefinitely postponed expanding the rental registry and considering a rent stabilization ordinance.
*   Approved several contracts for city services, including utility infrastructure, software, energy efficiency programs, parking enforcement, and water quality plant services.
*   Adopted amendments to city utility rules and regulations.

## Agenda Highlights

**1. Proclamation Honoring June 2026 as LGBTQIA+ Pride Month:** The Council formally recognized June 2026 as LGBTQIA+ Pride Month in Palo Alto, celebrating the community's contributions.

**2. Study Session on Flock Automated License Plate Recognition Technology:** The Council held a discussion to learn more about the potential use of automated license plate recognition (ALPR) technology in the city. No decisions were made.

**3. Approval of Minutes from May 11 and May 18, 2026 Meeting:** The Council approved the official records from its meetings held on May 11 and May 18, 2026.

**4. Indefinite Deferral of Rental Registry Expansion and Rent Stabilization Ordinance:** The Council decided to indefinitely postpone expanding the rental registry to smaller properties and considering a rent stabilization (rent control) ordinance.
