## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held a special meeting on April 15, 2026, focusing on a single action item. Council members met to discuss the potential temporary closure of the Churchill Avenue rail crossing and determine next steps, including its environmental review status. After discussion, the Council decided to postpone any action on this item.

Key actions and discussions:
*   The item regarding the potential temporary closure of the Churchill Avenue Rail Crossing was discussed.
*   No action was taken on this item.
*   The item was continued to the May 11, 2026 City Council meeting for further consideration.

## Agenda Highlights

**1. Expedited Evaluation of the Potential Temporary Closure of the Churchill Avenue Rail Crossing, Determination of Next Steps, and CEQA Status – Statutorily and Categorically Exempt.**
The City Council considered an expedited review of whether to temporarily close the Churchill Avenue rail crossing. This discussion also included determining the next steps for the project and its environmental review status under the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), which was noted as exempt from certain requirements. After deliberation, the Council decided not to take any action at this meeting. Instead, they chose to continue the discussion and decision-making on this important item to their meeting on May 11, 2026.
