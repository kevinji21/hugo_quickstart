## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held a regular meeting on February 9, 2026, addressing critical community concerns alongside routine city business. Public comments focused heavily on urgent rail safety measures and youth mental health, particularly regarding the Churchill crossing, as well as health concerns related to gas stoves. The Council also approved various administrative and financial items, and held a public hearing to finalize the list of properties for the annual weed abatement program.

*   Residents voiced strong concerns about rail safety, urging the closure of the Churchill crossing and the placement of guards, and also discussed health impacts of gas stoves.
*   The Rail Safety/Youth Mental Health Ad Hoc Committee reported on ongoing efforts with Caltrain and the school district to develop recommendations for youth safety and rail crossing mitigation.
*   The Council approved a Consent Calendar covering routine items such as meeting minutes, policy updates, vehicle purchases, and contract amendments for city projects.
*   An exception was granted to rehire a retired expert on a temporary, part-time basis for the Regional Water Quality Control Plant due to specialized needs.
*   A public hearing was held for the annual weed abatement program, with the Council approving the list of properties, noting Stanford University's successful removal of several properties due to compliance.

## Agenda Highlights

*   **Agenda Changes:** The meeting agenda was adjusted to include a closed session at the end and to swap the order of public comments and Council member remarks.
*   **Public Comment:** Many residents, including students and parents, spoke passionately about the urgent need for rail safety improvements, specifically advocating for the closure of the Churchill crossing and the deployment of paid guards. Concerns about the health effects of gas stoves and support for induction stove rebates were also raised. Members of the Palo Alto Youth Council shared updates on their mental health and civic engagement initiatives.
*   **Council Member Questions, Comments and Announcements:** Mayor Veenker acknowledged a recent youth loss and ongoing community response. Councilmember Lythcott-Haims provided an update from the Rail Safety/Youth Mental Health Ad Hoc Committee, detailing their collaboration with Caltrain and the school district to develop recommendations for youth safety, including exploring Churchill crossing closure and security guards.
*   **Consent Calendar (Items 1-8):** The Council approved a series of routine administrative and operational items. These included approving past meeting minutes, updating a retail preservation map, revising the city's investment policy, authorizing vehicle and equipment purchases, increasing funding for a sewer rehabilitation project, extending a facility use agreement for First Tee - Silicon Valley, amending a contract for California Avenue parklet plans, and approving contracts for on-call fire code and environmental services.
*   **Action Item 9: Rehire Retired Annuitant Aaron Miller:** The Council approved a resolution to temporarily rehire Aaron Miller, a retired employee, for a part-time, special-project assignment at the Regional Water Quality Control Plant. This decision was made to leverage his specific expertise for projects and training while the city works to fill the position permanently.
*   **Action Item 10: Public Hearing on Weed Abatement:** The Council conducted its annual public hearing regarding properties identified for weed abatement. Following the hearing, the Council approved the list of properties for the 2026 weed abatement program. Notably, Stanford University successfully requested the removal of four of its five properties from the list, demonstrating their proactive efforts in managing vegetation for fire prevention.
