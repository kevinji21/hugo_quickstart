## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held its regular meeting on April 6, 2026, addressing a range of city business from commission appointments to significant infrastructure projects and planning updates. Key discussions included future partnerships with local nonprofits and the ongoing development of the San Antonio Road Area Plan. The Council also approved several contracts for city services and infrastructure, and heard an appeal related to a residential development.

Key actions and discussions included:
*   Interviewing a candidate for a vacancy on the Planning & Transportation Commission.
*   Discussing improvements for the city's nonprofit partnership workplan for the upcoming fiscal year.
*   Providing feedback on draft land use and transportation concepts for the San Antonio Road Area Plan.
*   Approving contracts for turf replacement at El Camino Park and root treatment services for city pipes.
*   Approving a final map for a condominium subdivision on El Camino Real.
*   Extending and increasing funding for library materials and public art engineering services.
*   Hearing an appeal regarding a residential development project on Barron Avenue.
*   Holding a second reading for an ordinance to amend the city's zoning rules.

## Agenda Highlights

**1. Interview Candidate for Planning & Transportation Commission**
The Council conducted an interview with a candidate seeking appointment to a vacant position on the Planning & Transportation Commission, which advises the Council on land use and transportation matters.

**2. Discussion of Nonprofit Partnership Workplan Phase I**
Council members discussed the first phase of a plan to improve how the city partners with local nonprofit organizations, focusing on enhancements for the 2027 fiscal year. This was a study session to gather feedback on the proposed changes.

**3. San Antonio Road Area Plan Update**
The Council received an update on the San Antonio Road Area Plan and provided feedback on the draft ideas for future land use and transportation in that specific area of the city.

**4. Approval of Minutes**
The Council formally approved the minutes from its previous meetings held on March 16 and March 23, 2026.

**5. Approval of El Camino Park Turf Replacement Contract**
The Council approved a contract for up to $878,919 with FieldTurf USA, Inc. to replace the turf at El Camino Park and adjusted the city's budget for this capital improvement project.

**6. Approval of Root Foaming Treatment Services Contract**
A three-year contract for up to $279,098 was approved with Duke’s Root Control for services to treat tree roots that can interfere with city pipes and infrastructure.

**7. Approval of Final Map for Condominium Subdivision**
The Council approved the final map for properties at 4335 and 4345 El Camino Real. This approval allows for the creation of a condominium subdivision with a total of 29 units and included the naming of three private streets within the development.

**8. Approval of Library Materials Contracts Amendments**
Amendments were approved for contracts with Ingram Library Services, Midwest Tape, and OverDrive. These amendments increase the funding and extend the terms for the acquisition of print, media, and digital materials and services for the city's libraries.

**9. Approval of Structural Engineering Services Contract Amendment**
An amendment was approved for a contract with Hohbach-Lewin Inc., increasing the compensation by $10,000 and extending the term through March 30, 2029, for on-call structural engineering services related to public art installations.

**10. Appeal of Director’s Decision (530 Barron Avenue)**
The Council heard an appeal regarding a decision by the Director to deny an application for a residential project at 530 Barron Avenue. The project involved partially deconstructing an existing home and constructing a new two-story single-family residence, a Junior Accessory Dwelling Unit (JADU), and converting part of the existing house into a Detached Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU).

**11. SECOND READING: Ordinance Amending Title 18 (Zoning)**
The Council held a second reading for an ordinance that proposes amendments to Title 18 of the City's Zoning code, which governs land use and development regulations.
