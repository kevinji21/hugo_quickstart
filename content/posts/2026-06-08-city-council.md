## Summary
The Palo Alto City Council convened on June 8, 2026, for its regular meeting, addressing a variety of city operations, planning, and policy matters. The Council recognized two long-serving city employees upon their retirement, approved the upcoming municipal election, and adopted key plans for water management. Discussions also included city audits, infrastructure projects, and significant land use decisions.

Key actions and decisions included:
*   Recognizing Daniel Bergen and Julie Weiss for their service upon retirement.
*   Approving a resolution to call for a General Municipal Election on November 3, 2026.
*   Adopting the annual evaluation process for Council Appointed Officers.
*   Accepting reports on Utility Reserves and Public Safety Staffing and Overtime.
*   Approving a permanent Water Use Ordinance for the city.
*   Authorizing contracts for IT security, guardrail repair, and sanitary sewer inspection.
*   Upgrading the historic status of the property at 405 Kipling Street.
*   Approving the final map for the Buena Vista Mobile Home Park subdivision.
*   Adopting the 2025 Urban Water Management Plan and Water Shortage Contingency Plan after a public hearing.

## Agenda Highlights

**SPECIAL ORDERS OF THE DAY**
*   **Proclamation Expressing Appreciation to Daniel Bergen Upon Retirement:** The City Council formally recognized and thanked Daniel Bergen for his dedicated service to the city as he retires.
*   **Proclamation Expressing Appreciation to Julie Weiss Upon Retirement:** The City Council formally recognized and thanked Julie Weiss for her dedicated service to the city as she retires.

**CONSENT CALENDAR**
*   **Adoption of a Resolution Calling for a General Municipal Election to be held on November 3, 2026:** The Council approved the official resolution to hold the next General Municipal Election on November 3, 2026.
*   **Approval of FY26 Council Appointed Officers annual evaluation process:** The Council approved the timeline and specific questions to be used for the annual performance evaluations of city officers appointed by the Council.
*   **Accept City of Palo Alto Utility Reserves Advisory Report and Public Safety Staffing and Overtime Audit:** The Council accepted two important reports: one providing advice on the city's utility financial reserves and another auditing staffing levels and overtime within public safety departments.
*   **Approval of the City of Palo Alto's Permanent Water Use Ordinance:** The Council approved a permanent ordinance that establishes ongoing regulations for water use within Palo Alto.
*   **Approval of Purchase Order with Optiv Security for Varonis Systems:** The Council approved a three-year contract to purchase Varonis Systems software, which will enhance the city's IT security measures.
*   **Approval of Construction Contract with Valentine Corporation for Guardrail Repair Project:** The Council approved a contract for a project to repair guardrails in the city as part of its capital improvement program.
*   **Approval of Professional Services Contract with Presidio Systems, Inc. for CCTV Sanitary Sewer Pipeline Inspection:** The Council approved a three-year contract for specialized camera inspections of the city's sanitary sewer pipelines to assess their condition.
*   **Approval of Amendment No. 1 with Eight Professional Services Contracts for IT On-Call Services:** The Council approved increasing the funding and extending the term for eight existing contracts that provide on-call information technology services to the city.
*   **405 Kipling Street: Upgrade Historic Inventory Status:** The Council approved a change in the historic status of the property at 405 Kipling Street, upgrading it to a higher category on the local historic inventory due to its increased historical significance.
*   **3980 El Camino Real (Buena Vista): Request for a Final Map to Subdivide a Single Parcel:** The Council approved the final map to subdivide the Buena Vista Mobile Home Park property into two parcels. This action is a step towards renovating the mobile home park and building new affordable apartments on the site.

**ACTION ITEMS**
*   **PUBLIC HEARING: Adopt 2025 Urban Water Management Plan and 2025 Water Shortage Contingency Plan:** Following a public hearing, the Council adopted two crucial plans. The 2025 Urban Water Management Plan outlines how the city will manage its water resources, and the 2025 Water Shortage Contingency Plan details the actions the city will take during periods of water scarcity.
