## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held a special meeting on January 31, 2026, which was entirely dedicated to closed-door discussions. The Council met for several hours to address two confidential items concerning the City Attorney position: a potential appointment and labor negotiations. After returning to open session, Mayor Veenker announced that no reportable action was taken, meaning no public decisions or announcements were made regarding these matters at this meeting.

Key actions and discussion items:
*   The City Council convened a special meeting on January 31, 2026.
*   The entire meeting was held in Closed Session to discuss confidential personnel and labor negotiation matters.
*   Discussions focused on the appointment of the City Attorney and labor negotiations related to that position.
*   After the Closed Session, Mayor Veenker reported that no public action was taken by the Council.

## Agenda Highlights

**Closed Session Item 1: Public Employee Appointment (City Attorney)**
The Council went into a closed session to discuss the potential appointment of a new City Attorney. State law allows for these types of personnel discussions to be held confidentially to protect privacy and ensure effective deliberation.

**Closed Session Item 2: Conference with Labor Negotiators (City Attorney)**
Also in closed session, the Council conferred with labor negotiators regarding the City Attorney position. This discussion involved agency representatives Sandra Blanch and Nicholas Raisch, along with Teri Black from Teri Black & Co., focusing on negotiations for the unrepresented City Attorney employee.

**Outcome of Closed Session**
After nearly five and a half hours in closed session, the Council returned to open session. Mayor Veenker announced that there was no reportable action from the confidential discussions. This means that while discussions took place, no decisions were made public or formally voted on in a way that required immediate disclosure.
