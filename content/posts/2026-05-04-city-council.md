## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held its regular meeting on Monday, May 4, 2026, addressing a variety of community issues from housing and the city budget to transportation and local ordinances. Key discussions included the upcoming fiscal year's budget, while several important resolutions and contracts were approved through the consent calendar.

Key actions and discussions included:
*   Proclaimed May 2026 as Affordable Housing Month.
*   Reviewed the proposed Fiscal Year 2027 Operating and Capital Budgets.
*   Approved funding for local bikeway and transit projects.
*   Extended the lease for the Cubberley tennis facility until 2040.
*   Adopted ordinances to strengthen anti-discrimination policies and establish entertainment zones.

## Agenda Highlights

**SPECIAL ORDERS OF THE DAY**
*   **Proclamation of May 2026 as Affordable Housing Month:** The Council officially recognized May as Affordable Housing Month, emphasizing the importance of creating and supporting accessible housing options for all residents.

**CLOSED SESSION**
*   **Potential Litigation (156 North California Avenue):** Council members met privately with the City Attorney to discuss potential legal action concerning a property at 156 North California Avenue.
*   **Real Property Negotiations (4000 Middlefield Road - Cubberley Site):** The Council held a confidential discussion regarding ongoing negotiations with the Palo Alto Unified School District concerning the Cubberley Site, focusing on potential purchase, exchange, or lease terms.

**STUDY SESSION**
*   **Fiscal Year 2027 Proposed Operating and Capital Budgets:** The City Manager presented the initial draft of the budget for the next fiscal year, outlining proposed spending for city operations and major infrastructure projects. This was a presentation and discussion, not a final approval.

**CONSENT CALENDAR**
The Council approved a series of routine items in one motion, including:
*   **Approval of Minutes:** Approved the minutes from previous meetings held on April 13, April 15, and April 20, 2026.
*   **Transportation Development Act (TDA) Article 3 Funds Application:** Approved applying for $326,590 in state funds to support the South Palo Alto Bikeways Demonstration Project.
*   **Contract Amendment for Artwork (Stephen Galloway Studio):** Approved extending a contract with Stephen Galloway Studio until April 2029 for the design, fabrication, and installation of public artwork.
*   **Funding for Palo Alto Transit Center Quarry Road Connection:** Approved receiving up to $1 million from the Metropolitan Transportation Commission for improvements to the Quarry Road Connection at the Palo Alto Transit Center.
*   **Wetland Mitigation Monitoring Contract (Triple HS, INC):** Approved a five-year contract for nearly $400,000 with H. T. Harvey & Associates for ongoing monitoring and improvements related to wetland mitigation, along with a budget adjustment.
*   **Contract Amendment (Baker Tilly US, LLP):** Approved extending a contract with Baker Tilly US, LLP until June 30, 2028, with no additional cost, as recommended by a committee.
*   **Lease Amendment for Tennis Facility (Community Skating, Inc.):** Approved extending the lease with Community Skating, Inc. for the tennis facility at 3005 Middlefield Road until January 1, 2040.
*   **SECOND READING: Anti-Discrimination Ordinance Amendment:** The Council passed the second reading of an ordinance to amend the City's anti-discrimination policy, officially adding "Local CEDAW Principles" and a "Private Right of Action."
*   **SECOND READING: Entertainment Zones Ordinance:** The Council passed the second reading of an ordinance to create new rules for "Entertainment Zones" and establish one on a portion of California Avenue.

**ACTION ITEMS**
*   **Update and Provide Direction to Staff:** The agenda for this item was incomplete, so the specific topic discussed or direction given to staff is not available from the provided information.
