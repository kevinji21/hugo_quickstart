## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held a regular meeting on June 15, 2026, addressing key financial matters for the upcoming fiscal year, including utility rates and the city's overall budget. The Council also recognized a retiring staff member, approved a water shortage report, advanced a railroad safety project, and moved forward with an application for a state housing program.

Key actions and discussions included:
*   Honoring Bill Gutoff for his retirement.
*   Conducting public hearings and adopting resolutions for Fiscal Year 2027 utility rate changes across water, wastewater, refuse, electric, gas, and storm water services.
*   Adopting the city's Operating and Capital Budgets, municipal fee schedule, and appropriations limit for Fiscal Year 2027.
*   Approving an agreement for repairs and drainage improvements at the Roth Building for the Palo Alto Museum.
*   Approving the 2026 Annual Water Shortage Assessment Report.
*   Approving the conceptual design for the Alma Street-Charleston Road railroad crossing safety project.
*   Authorizing the submission of the city's Prohousing Designation Program application to the state.
*   Adopting amended salary schedules for various city employee unions.

## Agenda Highlights

**1. Proclamation Expressing Appreciation to Bill Gutoff Upon His Retirement**
The Council issued a proclamation to thank Bill Gutoff for his service to the city upon his retirement.

**2. PUBLIC HEARING & PROPOSITION 218 HEARING: Utility Rate Changes for Fiscal Year 2027**
Following a public hearing, the Council adopted resolutions to approve financial forecasts and make changes to the rate schedules for water, wastewater collection, refuse, electric, gas, fiber optic, and storm water utilities for the upcoming Fiscal Year 2027. This included directing funds to help reduce solar and energy storage installation costs for electric customers.

**3. PUBLIC HEARING: Fiscal Year 2027 Budget, Fees, and Museum Agreement**
The Council held a public hearing and adopted an ordinance approving the city's Operating and Capital Budgets, a revised organizational structure, and the municipal fee schedule for Fiscal Year 2027. They also set the appropriations limit for the year, accepted a report on retiree healthcare benefits, and authorized an agreement with the Palo Alto Museum for cost-sharing on repairs to the Roth Building and improvements to Heritage Park drainage.

**4. Approval of Minutes from June 1, 2026 Meeting**
The Council approved the official record of their meeting held on June 1, 2026.

**5. Approve Submittal of the 2026 Annual Water Shortage Assessment Report**
The Council approved the submission of the annual report that assesses the city's water supply and demand.

**6. Approve Conceptual Design for Alma Street-Charleston Road Railroad Crossing Safety Improvement Project**
The Council approved the initial design for safety improvements at the Alma Street-Charleston Road railroad crossing and directed staff to proceed with the final design and environmental analysis.

**7. Adoption of a Resolution Authorizing the City Manager to Submit the City’s Prohousing Designation Program Application**
The Council adopted a resolution allowing the City Manager to submit an application to the California Department of Housing and Community Development for the state's Prohousing Designation Program, which recognizes cities that are actively working to address housing needs.

**8. Adoption of Amended Salary Schedules for Service Employees‘ International Union, Utilities Management and Professional Association of Palo Alto, International Association of Fi...**
The Council adopted updated salary schedules for employees represented by various unions, including the Service Employees' International Union and the Utilities Management and Professional Association of Palo Alto.
