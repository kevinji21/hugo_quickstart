## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held a special meeting on February 23, 2026, addressing a range of city business from potential legal matters to significant budget adjustments and infrastructure maintenance. The Council spent considerable time in a closed session discussing potential litigation and approved several contracts for essential city services. A major focus was the mid-year budget review, which included significant financial decisions regarding city reserves and pension payments.

Key actions and discussions included:
*   A closed session was held to discuss potential litigation concerning 156 North California Avenue, with no reportable action taken.
*   The Oversized Vehicles (OSV) Ad Hoc Committee provided a status report, but no action was taken.
*   Approved several contracts for city operations, including upgrades to the Maybell Electric Substation, water leak detection services, fire watch at City Hall, and track watch services at grade crossings.
*   Approved amendments to the Fiscal Year 2026 budget, consolidated city reserves, and authorized a $10 million additional payment to the CalPERS pension fund.
*   Discussion on City Council Values and 2026 Objectives was deferred to a later date.

## Agenda Highlights

**1. Conference with City Attorney - Potential Litigation**
The Council met in a closed session to discuss potential litigation related to a property at 156 North California Avenue. After the discussion, the Mayor announced that no reportable action was taken.

**2. Status Report from the Oversized Vehicles (OSV) Ad Hoc Committee**
The committee formed to address issues related to oversized vehicles presented an update on their work. The Council received the report but did not take any specific action at this meeting.

**Consent Calendar (Items 3-7)**
The Council approved a group of routine items together without separate discussion. These included:
*   **Approval of Minutes:** Minutes from four previous City Council meetings in January and February 2026 were approved.
*   **Maybell Electric Substation Upgrade:** Approved a purchase order of up to $389,862 with ABB Inc. for equipment and services to upgrade ten circuit breakers at the Maybell Electric Substation.
*   **Water System Leak Detection:** Approved a three-year contract for up to $642,609 with M.E. Simpson Co., Inc. to conduct leak detection surveys for the city's water system.
*   **City Hall Fire Watch Services:** Approved an amendment to a contract with Universal Security Company, adding $80,915 for fire watch services at City Hall, bringing the new total to $301,667, along with a budget adjustment.
*   **Track Watch Services at Grade Crossings:** Approved an amendment to a contract with Orion Security, adding $1,623,314 for track watch services at four railroad grade crossings, for a new total of $1,708,314. This also included authorizing a cost-sharing agreement with the Palo Alto Unified School District for $854,157 and a budget adjustment.

**8. FY 2026 Mid-Year Budget Review**
The Council conducted its mid-year review of the city's budget for Fiscal Year 2026. They approved several amendments to the budget for various city funds and capital projects. Additionally, the Council decided to combine the remaining $6.0 million in the "Uncertainty Reserve" with the "Budget Stabilization Reserve" to simplify financial management. Finally, they authorized a $10.0 million additional payment from the city's pension trust to CalPERS, the state's public employee retirement system.

**9. Approval of City Council Values, 2026 Objectives, Ad Hoc Purpose Statements, and Committee Workplans**
This item, which included discussions on the Council's guiding values and future goals, was not heard during this meeting and has been postponed to an uncertain future date.
