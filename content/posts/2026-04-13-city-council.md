## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council convened on April 13, 2026, for its regular meeting, addressing a variety of city matters from commission appointments to significant development proposals. Key discussions included a public hearing for a large multi-family housing project on El Camino Real, the adoption of the Council's core values and 2026 objectives, and strategies to mitigate the impacts of combining residential properties.

*   Appointed members to the Human Relations, Planning & Transportation, and Utilities Advisory Commissions.
*   Issued proclamations for National Animal Control Officer Appreciation Week and National Public Safety Telecommunicators Week.
*   Approved a contract amendment for the Local Advanced Water Purification System Project, increasing its budget and extending its term.
*   Held a public hearing for a proposed 321-unit, seven-story multi-family housing development at 3606 El Camino Real, including affordable units.
*   Adopted the City Council's core values, 2026 objectives, and committee work plans.
*   Discussed a memo on mitigating the impacts of combining residential properties into larger lots.

## Agenda Highlights

1.  **Appointments to City Commissions:** The Council appointed new members to the Human Relations Commission, Planning & Transportation Commission, and Utilities Advisory Commission.
2.  **National Animal Control Officer Appreciation Week:** The Council issued a proclamation recognizing the vital work of animal control officers from April 12-18, 2026.
3.  **National Public Safety Telecommunicators Week:** A proclamation was issued to honor the essential services provided by public safety dispatchers from April 12-18, 2026.
4.  **Easement Vacation at 700 Hansen Way:** The Council adopted a resolution to remove a public service easement at 700 Hansen Way.
5.  **Advanced Water Purification System Project Contract Amendment:** An amendment was approved for the contract with Black & Veatch, Inc., for the Local Advanced Water Purification System Project. This increases the project budget by $963,160 and extends the contract term to December 31, 2028.
6.  **SAP On-Call Services Contract Amendments:** The City Manager was authorized to amend eight professional services contracts for SAP on-call support, increasing the total amount by $2,000,000 and extending the term for one year.
7.  **Emergency Standby Council Appointment:** The 2026 Emergency Standby Council members were appointed.
8.  **Public Hearing for 3606 El Camino Real Development:** The Council held a public hearing to consider the approval of a major architectural review for a seven-story, 321-unit multi-family residential housing development at 3606 El Camino Real. This project includes affordable housing units and is proposed under the state's "Builder's Remedy" provision.
9.  **City Council Values and 2026 Objectives:** Following a previous discussion, the Council approved its core values, strategic objectives for 2026, statements for ad hoc committees, and work plans for its standing committees.
10. **Mitigating Property Aggregation Impacts:** The Council discussed a memo from colleagues regarding potential strategies to address and lessen the impacts when multiple residential properties are combined into larger parcels.
A.  **RV Safe Parking Program Update:** An informational report was provided to the Council detailing the semiannual update on the expansion of the recreational vehicle safe parking program at 2000 Geng Road and outlining its financial needs.
