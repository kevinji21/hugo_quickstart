## Summary
The Palo Alto City Council held a regular meeting on May 18, 2026, addressing a range of city business from recognizing cultural heritage to major development plans. Key decisions included advancing the Cubberley Project with a conceptual master plan and a proposed sales tax measure, approving a significant residential subdivision on Old Trace Road, and adding a specific lot on California Avenue to the city's housing inventory. The Council also handled several routine administrative approvals and contracts for city services and infrastructure.

**Key Actions and Decisions:**
*   Recognized May as Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander (AANHPI) Heritage Month and AANHPI Mental Health Awareness Day.
*   Adopted the Cubberley Conceptual Master Plan and directed staff to pursue a one-half-cent sales tax ballot measure for the November 2026 election to help fund the project.
*   Approved a tentative map to subdivide a 1.02-acre lot at 4103 Old Trace Road, paving the way for nine new single-family homes and seven junior accessory dwelling units.
*   Directed the Planning and Development Services Director to add Lot A (APN 124-28-045) at 156 California Avenue to the City's Housing Element Sites Inventory.
*   Approved various consent calendar items, including minutes, a quarterly update on Council priorities, vacating a utility easement, funding for street maintenance, suspending the Downtown BID assessment, and extending an organized retail theft grant.
*   Approved construction contracts for a new modular restroom building at Mitchell Park and for the Storm Drainage System Replacement and Rehabilitation Project.
*   Approved an amendment to extend the contract with Stanford University for public safety dispatch services.
*   Deferred an ordinance related to retail vitality policies and zoning changes.

## Agenda Highlights

**Special Orders of the Day**
*   **AANHPI Heritage Month Recognition:** The Council formally recognized May as Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander (AANHPI) Heritage Month and May 10, 2026, as AANHPI Mental Health Awareness Day.

**Consent Calendar**
*   **Meeting Minutes:** Approved the minutes from the May 4, 2026, meeting.
*   **Council Priorities Update:** Received an update on the Council's priorities and objectives for the first quarter of 2026.
*   **Utility Easement Vacation:** Approved vacating a portion of a public utility easement at 590 Maybell Avenue.
*   **Road Repair Funding:** Adopted a resolution for Senate Bill 1, providing the project list for street maintenance for Fiscal Year 2027.
*   **Downtown BID Assessment:** Approved suspending the special assessment for the Downtown Palo Alto Business Improvement District for Fiscal Year 2027.
*   **Organized Retail Theft Grant:** Approved extending a grant agreement with the California Board of State and Community Corrections for organized retail theft prevention by one year.
*   **Mitchell Park Restroom Contract:** Approved a construction contract of $261,600 for site work for a new modular restroom building at Mitchell Park.
*   **Storm Drainage Project Contract:** Approved a construction contract of $544,556 for the Storm Drainage System Replacement and Rehabilitation Project.
*   **Stanford Dispatch Services Contract:** Approved extending the contract with Stanford University for public safety dispatch services until September 30, 2027.

**Action Items**
*   **Cubberley Project:** The Council adopted the Cubberley Conceptual Master Plan and its environmental review. They also directed staff to pursue a one-half-cent sales tax ballot measure for the November 2026 election to help fund the project.
*   **156 California Avenue Housing:** The Council directed staff to add Lot A (APN 124-28-045) at 156 California Avenue to the City’s Housing Element Sites Inventory.
*   **Retail Vitality Ordinance:** An ordinance to amend building and zoning regulations to implement retail vitality policies was deferred to an uncertain future date.
*   **4103 Old Trace Road Subdivision:** The Council approved a tentative map to subdivide a vacant 1.02-acre lot at 4103 Old Trace Road. This approval allows for the creation of nine residential lots and one private street, facilitating the construction of nine new single-family residences and seven junior accessory dwelling units.
