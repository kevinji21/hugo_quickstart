*This summary is based on action minutes. Full summary minutes were not available for this meeting.*

## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held a regular meeting on April 20, 2026, addressing a range of topics from ceremonial proclamations to significant policy changes and project decisions. Key discussions included the regional plan to end homelessness and a proposed rezoning for a new residential development. The Council approved a consent calendar with several routine items and contract amendments, and made important decisions regarding public meeting procedures to enhance accessibility and participation.

Key actions and decisions included:
*   Recognizing Earth Day and National Crime Victims' Rights Week.
*   Discussing the draft Santa Clara County Community Plan to End Homelessness.
*   Reviewing a request to rezone 910 Webster Street for a 70-unit residential building.
*   Approving minor modifications to the City's Housing Element site inventory.
*   Advancing an ordinance to establish an Entertainment Zone on California Avenue.
*   Approving funding and term extensions for the City's phone system maintenance and a residential water heater replacement program.
*   Declining a proposed amendment for modifications to an assisted living facility at 4075 El Camino Way.
*   Establishing the Embarcadero Road Improvement Project, Phase 1.
*   Upholding an order to demolish buildings at 531 Stanford Avenue.
*   Adopting changes to meeting procedures to improve remote public participation, translation services, and public outreach.

## Agenda Highlights

**Special Orders of the Day**
*   **Earth Day Proclamation:** The Council recognized Earth Day and the Palo Alto Youth Climate Advisory Board. No formal action was taken beyond the recognition.
*   **National Crime Victims' Rights Week Proclamation:** The Council recognized National Crime Victims' Rights Week. No formal action was taken beyond the recognition.

**Study Session**
*   **2026-2030 Santa Clara County Community Plan to End Homelessness:** Council members discussed the draft plan for addressing homelessness in Santa Clara County. This was an informational discussion, and no decisions were made.
*   **910 Webster Street Prescreening:** The Council discussed a preliminary request to rezone the property to allow for the construction of 70 residential units in a seven-story building. This was a discussion item, and no formal action was taken.

**Consent Calendar**
*   **Minutes Approval:** The minutes from the April 6, 2026, meeting were approved.
*   **Housing Element Site Inventory Modifications:** The Council authorized the Director of Planning and Development Services to make minor adjustments to the 2023-2031 Housing Element site inventory.
*   **Phone System Maintenance Contract:** An amendment was approved to increase funding by $212,819 and extend the contract term to June 30, 2027, for the maintenance of the City’s enterprise phone system.
*   **Entertainment Zone Ordinance (First Read):** The Council adopted, on first reading, an ordinance to create an Entertainment Zone on California Avenue, as recommended by the Economic Development Ad Hoc Committee. This will require a second reading for final adoption.
*   **Water Heater Replacement Program Contract:** An amendment was approved to increase funding by $950,000 (for a total of $1,445,000) and extend the contract term for one year, ending July 21, 2027, for the Residential Emergency Water Heater Replacement Pilot Program.
*   **4075 El Camino Way Project:** The Council declined to adopt an ordinance that would have allowed modifications to an existing 121-unit assisted living and memory care facility. This means the proposed modifications were not approved.
*   **Embarcadero Road Improvement Project, Phase 1:** The Council amended the FY 2026 Budget to establish this project and authorized the City Manager to award a construction contract for it.

**Action Items**
*   **531 Stanford Avenue Demolition Appeal:** The Council denied an appeal, thereby upholding the Chief Building Official's order to demolish the buildings located at 531 Stanford Avenue.
*   **Meeting Procedures Changes:** The Council adopted several recommendations to update meeting procedures:
    *   They decided against using expanded alternative teleconferencing for Boards, Commissions, and Committees.
    *   A new default practice was adopted for public comment, where in-person general public comment will be taken near the beginning of the agenda, and remote general public comment at the end, with the Chair having discretion to adjust. This practice will be reevaluated later.
    *   A Two-Way Remote Public Access Disruption Policy was approved.
    *   Practices were approved to assist with third-party translation and interpretation services, including arranging space for interpreters, providing extra time for speakers using interpretation, and allowing or loaning devices for translation.
    *   The City Clerk was directed to implement new outreach practices, such as sharing agenda newsletters with community groups, posting informational resources with QR codes in public facilities, and translating the Meeting Participation Guide into the two most commonly spoken non-English languages.
