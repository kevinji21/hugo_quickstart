## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held a regular meeting on March 9, 2026, addressing a range of city business from ceremonial recognitions to strategic planning and zoning discussions. Key actions included approving the Council's 2026 objectives and workplans, discussing potential zoning changes for historic buildings, and receiving important reports on local policing data and Stanford University Medical Center operations.

Key actions and discussions included:
*   Recognizing March 2026 as Youth Arts Month with a proclamation.
*   Holding a study session to prescreen a request for zoning changes to benefit historic buildings.
*   Approving the City Council's values, 2026 objectives, and committee workplans.
*   Amending a contract to continue work on the City's Bicycle and Pedestrian Transportation Plan Update.
*   Receiving the annual report from Stanford University Medical Center for Fiscal Year 2025.
*   Reviewing an informational report on Racial and Identity Profiling Act (RIPA) data from the National Policing Institute.
*   Discussing the appointment of a new City Attorney in closed session.

## Agenda Highlights

**CLOSED SESSION**
*   **AA1. Public Employee Appointment - City Attorney:** The Council met privately to discuss the appointment of a new City Attorney.

**SPECIAL ORDERS OF THE DAY**
*   **1. Proclamation Recognizing March 2026 as Youth Arts Month:** The Council issued a formal proclamation to celebrate and acknowledge March 2026 as Youth Arts Month in Palo Alto.

**STUDY SESSION**
*   **2. 51 Encina Avenue - Zoning Text Amendment Prescreening:** The Council held an initial discussion about a proposal to change city zoning rules. The proposed changes would allow certain historic buildings in the Commercial Service (CS) zone to have more floor area and transfer development rights, aiming to support the preservation and use of these buildings.

**CONSENT CALENDAR**
*   **3. Approval of Minutes from February 23, 2026 Meeting:** The minutes from a previous Council meeting were approved.
*   **4. Appointment of 2026 Emergency Standby Council:** Council members were appointed to serve as the Emergency Standby Council for 2026, ready to act in case of a city emergency.
*   **5. Adoption of a Resolution Setting Committee Meeting Times:** A resolution was adopted to set the regular meeting times for the Finance Committee at 4:00 PM and the Policy & Services Committee at 6:00 PM.
*   **6. Stanford University Medical Center (SUMC) Annual Report for FY 2025:** The Council received the annual report detailing operations and compliance from Stanford University Medical Center for the past fiscal year.
*   **7. Approval of Professional Services Contract for Green Stormwater Infrastructure Project:** This item was removed from the agenda.
*   **8. Approval of Contract Amendment for Bicycle and Pedestrian Transportation Plan Update:** An amendment was approved to extend a contract and increase funding for Kittelson & Associates, Inc., allowing them to continue work on updating the City's Bicycle and Pedestrian Transportation Plan.
*   **9. FIRST READING: Ordinance for Noncomplying Facility Exceptions:** The Council held the first reading of a proposed ordinance that would add minor exceptions for facilities that don't fully comply with current zoning rules, often referred to as "de minimis" exceptions.

**ACTION ITEMS**
*   **10. Update on Senate Bill 79 and Downtown Housing Plan:** This item was removed from the agenda.
*   **11. Approval of City Council Values, 2026 Objectives, Ad Hoc Purpose Statements, and Committee Workplans:** The Council approved its core values, set its strategic objectives for 2026, defined the purpose of its temporary (ad hoc) committees, and adopted the workplans for its standing committees.

**CLOSED SESSION**
*   **12. CONFERENCE WITH LEGAL COUNSEL—POTENTIAL LITIGATION:** The Council met privately with legal counsel to discuss four potential litigation cases.

**INFORMATION REPORTS**
*   **A. Informational Report: National Policing Institute Regarding Palo Alto's RIPA Data:** The Council received a report from the National Policing Institute that analyzed Palo Alto's data collected under the Racial and Identity Profiling Act of 2015. This report was for informational purposes only, with no action taken.
