## Summary
The Palo Alto City Council held a special meeting on May 11, 2026, addressing a range of city business from recognizing public service and community heritage to discussing the upcoming fiscal year budget and considering significant development and infrastructure projects. Key discussions included a large multi-family housing proposal and the potential temporary closure of the Churchill Avenue rail crossing.

Key actions and discussion items included:
*   Recognizing city employees for their service and proclaiming Public Service Recognition Week, along with National Police Week.
*   Discussing the proposed Fiscal Year 2027 Operating and Capital Budget.
*   Approving contracts for software maintenance and a utility customer portal.
*   Holding a public hearing for a 183-unit multi-family housing development on El Camino Real.
*   Continuing discussions on the potential temporary closure of the Churchill Avenue rail crossing.

## Agenda Highlights

**Special Orders of the Day**
The Council honored city employees with Years of Service Awards and issued a proclamation for Public Service Recognition Week. A proclamation was also made to recognize National Police Week and National Peace Officers' Memorial Day. An item to recognize Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander (AANHPI) Heritage Month was removed from the agenda.

**Closed Session**
Council members met privately with legal counsel to discuss existing litigation concerning "Anne Kramer vs City of Palo Alto." No public action was taken during this session.

**Study Session**
**FY 2027 Proposed Operating and Capital Budget - Finance Committee Update and Council Budget Discussion:** The Council received an update from the Finance Committee and engaged in a detailed discussion about the city's proposed budget for the next fiscal year, covering both day-to-day operations and major infrastructure projects. Public comments were also heard on this item.

**Consent Calendar**
Items on the Consent Calendar are typically approved together without separate discussion unless a Council member requests otherwise.
*   **Zscaler Software Maintenance and Support:** The Council approved a purchase order for $329,886 to Solutions Simplified for three years of Zscaler software maintenance and support.
*   **WaterSmart Utilities Customer Portal Contract Amendment:** An amendment was approved to extend the contract with VERTEXONE SOFTWARE, LLC for the WaterSmart Utilities Customer Portal by three years and increase funding by $140,000, bringing the total to $677,763.

**Action Items**
*   **3781 El Camino Real Multi-Family Housing Development:** The Council held a public hearing for a request to merge four parcels and construct a seven-story, 183-unit multi-family residential housing development. Thirteen percent of these units are proposed to be affordable for low-income residents, in accordance with the Builder’s Remedy state law. Staff and the applicant presented, and public comments were received.
*   **Churchill Avenue Rail Crossing Temporary Closure:** The Council continued its discussion from a previous meeting regarding the potential temporary closure of the Churchill Avenue rail crossing. This session focused on further deliberation and determining next steps, with no additional public testimony heard.

**Information Reports**
The Council received informational updates on the Palo Alto Dark Fiber Expansion Plan and Fiber-to-the-Premises, as well as the Palo Alto Transportation Management Association's 2025 Annual Report, 2027-29 Strategic Plan, and 2025 Commute Survey. These items were for information only and did not involve Council action or discussion during the meeting.
