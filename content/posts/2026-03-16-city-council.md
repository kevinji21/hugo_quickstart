## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held a special meeting on March 16, 2026, addressing a range of city business from ceremonial recognitions to significant development approvals and key personnel appointments. The Council appointed a new City Attorney and approved a major housing project proposed under state law, alongside several routine contracts and financial reports.

Key actions and discussions included:
*   Appointed Christopher Jensen as the new City Attorney.
*   Approved a large housing development at 2100-2400 Geng Road, including 145 townhomes with a portion designated for low-income households, proposed under the state's Builder's Remedy law.
*   Recognized the Sister City Tsuchiura, Japan student exchange program and proclaimed March 2026 as American Red Cross Month.
*   Approved various contracts for city operations, including new radios, an HR platform, electricity agreements, engineering consulting, legal services, and a stormwater infrastructure project.
*   Accepted the city's annual financial audit report for Fiscal Year 2025.

## Agenda Highlights

**SPECIAL ORDERS OF THE DAY**
*   **Item 1 (Sister City Recognition):** The Council recognized participants in the student exchange program with Palo Alto's Sister City, Tsuchiura, Japan.
*   **Item 2 (American Red Cross Month):** A proclamation was issued, recognizing March 2026 as American Red Cross Month in Palo Alto.

**CONSENT CALENDAR**
*   **Item 3 (Meeting Minutes):** The minutes from the March 2, 2026 meeting were approved.
*   **Item 4 (Motorola Radios Lease):** The Council approved a lease-purchase agreement not exceeding $5.8 million for new multi-band portable and mobile radios for all city departments.
*   **Item 5 (Electricity Agreements):** Resolutions were adopted to approve master power purchase and sale agreements with several energy companies for electricity-related commodities and services, delegating authority to the City Manager for transactions.
*   **Item 6 (NEOGOV Contract):** A professional services contract not exceeding $686,277 was approved with NEOGOV for a new human resources platform covering recruitment, applicant tracking, and employee development.
*   **Item 7 (Flynn Resources Contract Amendment):** An amendment was approved to increase funding by $170,000 and extend a contract for two years with Flynn Resources Inc. for electric engineering consulting services.
*   **Item 8 (Legal Services Contract):** Authorization was given to fully encumber a legal services contract with Atkinson Andelson Loya Ruud & Romo for a total not-to-exceed amount of $255,000.
*   **Item 9 (Single Audit Report):** The Single Audit Report for the fiscal year ended June 30, 2025, conducted by Macias Gini & O’Connell, was accepted.
*   **Item 10 (Geosyntec Consultants Contract):** A professional services contract not exceeding $795,965 was approved with Geosyntec Consultants, Inc. for design and outreach services for the City Park-Green Stormwater Infrastructure Project.

**ACTION ITEMS**
*   **Item 11 (City Attorney Appointment):** The Council approved an employment agreement and adopted a resolution appointing Christopher Jensen as the new City Attorney, with an annual salary of $380,000.
*   **Item 12 (Geng Road Housing Project):** The Council considered and approved applications for a major housing development at 2100-2400 Geng Road. The project involves constructing 145 for-sale townhome units, with 13% of these units deed-restricted for low-income households. This project was proposed in accordance with the state's Builder’s Remedy law and included the adoption of a Parkland Improvement Ordinance to authorize necessary tree removals.
