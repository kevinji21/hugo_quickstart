## Summary

The Palo Alto City Council held its annual retreat on January 24, 2026, to discuss and begin the process of selecting its key priorities for the upcoming year and consider the formation of ad hoc committees. Facilitated by Amy Howorth, the meeting included an orientation, public input on various community concerns, and extensive discussion among Councilmembers about their shared values, potential focus areas, and how to effectively define and pursue their goals for 2026.

Key discussion items included:
*   Councilmembers shared personal insights and surprises from their time serving on the Council.
*   Public comments addressed topics such as increasing pickleball court capacity, affordable housing, biodiversity, natural grass fields, microplastic pollution, citizen engagement, and climate change initiatives.
*   The Council engaged in a detailed discussion about the distinction between enduring City values, annual priorities, and specific objectives, and how these guide staff work.
*   Individual Councilmembers proposed a range of potential priorities for 2026, including reducing greenhouse gas emissions, fostering economic development, achieving a pro-housing designation, improving operational efficiency, advancing the Cubberley Project, and optimizing electric utility services.
*   Council debated whether to maintain existing broad priorities or narrow them to focus on more urgent, specific initiatives.

## Agenda Highlights

**1. Overview and orientation to retreat focus**
The retreat began with an introduction by City Manager Ed Shikada and facilitator Amy Howorth, outlining the day's goal: to discuss and select the 2026 City Council Priorities and ad hoc committees. Councilmembers then shared what had surprised them most about their roles, touching on aspects like the caliber of colleagues and staff, the difference between campaigning and collegiality, the broad scope of Palo Alto's work, and the dedication of public servants.

**Public Comment**
Residents provided input on several community issues:
*   **Recreation:** Multiple speakers advocated for converting tennis courts to pickleball courts to enhance community wellness.
*   **Accountability:** One resident suggested disclosing annual goals and performance indicators for city management and Council-appointed offices.
*   **Housing:** Affordable housing was urged as a top priority, with a call to follow up on potential housing sites.
*   **Environment:** Requests included integrating biodiversity into sustainability goals and parks projects, conducting a pilot study for natural grass fields, and developing a project to reduce microplastic and "forever chemical" pollution.
*   **Citizen Engagement:** Concerns were raised about citizen frustration regarding a perceived lack of involvement and ignored residential wishes, along with issues related to Station 4 and the new city attorney.
*   **Broad Priorities:** Other comments emphasized climate change reduction, affordable housing, creek renaturalization, pesticide control, native plants, services for the unhoused, and consensus building.

**A. Discussion and selection of Priorities and methods to prioritize and ensure progress**
This was a major focus of the retreat. The Council discussed the framework for setting priorities, distinguishing between long-term values and annual priorities. Councilmembers individually put forward their top priorities for the year. These included reducing greenhouse gas emissions, boosting economic development, securing a state pro-housing designation, enhancing operational efficiency, making progress on specific areas like Cal Ave and Ramona, advancing the Cubberley Project, focusing on near-term housing milestones, and optimizing the electric utility. There was a significant discussion on whether to keep the existing four broad priorities or to narrow the focus to a smaller number of more urgent items. The City Manager clarified that Council priorities help staff understand which areas require special attention beyond their ongoing responsibilities.

**B. Discussion of Council ad hoc committees and other strategies to achieve goals**
While listed on the agenda as a topic for discussion, the provided minutes primarily focused on the extensive debate around defining and selecting the 2026 Council priorities. Specific details regarding the formation or discussion of ad hoc committees were not elaborated upon in this segment of the minutes.

**C. Next Steps**
The minutes conclude mid-discussion, without detailing specific next steps decided upon during this retreat session.
